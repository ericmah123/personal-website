## Priority Issues

### [P2] Flip card affordance gap persists on touch devices
The rotate hint icon (opacity: 0 → 0.5 on hover) never fires on touch screens. Mobile visitors still see flat, opaque cards with no cue that they're interactive.

**Why it matters:** A recruiter on their phone taps "Experience" in the nav, sees cards with company names and dates, and moves on without seeing role descriptions.

**Fix:** Use `@media (hover: none)` in CSS to show the hint at full opacity on touch devices. Lowest-effort correct fix. Alternatively add a faint "tap to reveal" text label at opacity 0.35 at rest.

**Suggested command:** /impeccable polish

### [P3] Interaction inconsistency: hover-reveal (Projects) vs click-flip (Timeline)
Same goal, two interaction models. Visitors who used hover on project cards will try hover on timeline cards and see nothing.

**Fix:** Switch timeline cards to hover-flip. Click-flip was chosen to avoid accidental flips, but the cards are large enough that accidental hover is unlikely.

**Suggested command:** /impeccable polish

### [P3] Nav toggle has no keyboard role or handler
The nav element has onClick for toggle but no role="button", and no onKeyDown. aria-expanded is on the nav landmark, not a button. Keyboard users cannot toggle the nav open.

**Fix:** Wrap dots icon in a button with aria-expanded and aria-label="Toggle navigation". Move click/keyboard handlers to the button element.

**Suggested command:** /impeccable audit

### [P3] Prefers-reduced-motion users miss nav entrance animation
The intro timer is correctly gated but these users still face the original nav discovery problem. The fix improved discovery for most users but leaves the reduced-motion subset behind.

**Fix:** For prefers-reduced-motion users, show a persistent micro-label ("menu") beside the dots at low opacity in the collapsed state, or open/close the nav instantly (no animation) so they still see the links.

**Suggested command:** /impeccable polish
