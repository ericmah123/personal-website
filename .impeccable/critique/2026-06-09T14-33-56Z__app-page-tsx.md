## Overall Impression

Strong design with a real point of view. The portfolio's primary goal (get Eric hired) has two specific gaps: timeline descriptions are invisible without knowing to click cards, and there is no contact CTA above the footer.

## What's Working

1. Project cards' problem/solution hover-reveal — inventive and brand-appropriate.
2. Glass system is earned, not decorative — backdrop-filter justifies the effect.
3. Intro panel copy + CNRL chip — dry, specific voice carried through interaction detail.

## Priority Issues

### [P1] Flip card interaction has zero affordance
Back face content (experience descriptions) invisible without prior knowledge. Recruiters miss all timeline descriptions. Fix: add a "click to flip" indicator, switch to hover-flip, or surface a brief description excerpt on the front face.

### [P1] No contact path visible above the footer
GitHub and LinkedIn icons in the footer are the only contact options — unlabeled, at the bottom of a long scroll. Fix: add email or a "get in touch" CTA in the hero or footer text.

### [P2] Navigation starts fully hidden with no affordance
Glass pill with dots icon requires discovery. First-time visitors may not recognize it as navigation. Fix: entrance animation that briefly shows expanded state, or small text label beside the dots.

### [P2] Section labels on all three content sections — uniform cadence weakens the motif
"Intro," "Experience," "Projects" all open with the section-label eyebrow. Fix: let one section breathe without it, or vary the heading cadence.

### [P3] Interaction inconsistency: hover-reveal (Projects) vs click-flip (Timeline)
Same interaction goal, two different models on the same page. Fix: switch timeline to hover-flip.

## Persona Red Flags

**Taylor (Fast-Scanning Recruiter):** Misses all timeline content. Contact friction at end of positive visit.

**Jordan (Confused First-Timer):** Nav discovery delayed 5-10s. Flip card content entirely missed.

**Casey (Distracted Mobile User):** Top-center nav awkward for one-handed use. Contact path requires understanding unlabeled icons.
