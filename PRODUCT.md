# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two audiences who arrive with different intent. Recruiters and hiring managers land first — they need a fast read: who Eric is, what he's done, why he's worth a conversation. Technical peers go deeper, reading project detail and Eric's Corner posts to get a feel for how he thinks. The site must satisfy both without compromising for either.

## Product Purpose

Personal portfolio and blog for Eric Mah — CS grad, incoming Technical Business Analyst at CNRL. It exists to make a strong first impression on recruiters, give engineers something to dig into, and let Eric's personality through in the details (Eric's Corner, the intro panel, the corner motifs). Success is a recruiter who wants to reach out and an engineer who wants to keep reading.

## Brand Personality

Sharp, human, curious. The structure is intentional and confident — nothing is there by accident. The copy and "corner" moments let real personality through. Eric is not performing seniority; he's showing how he thinks.

## Anti-references

- The generic dev portfolio scaffold: React hero with avatar, skills-progress-bar grid, flat card timeline, three identical project tiles. Avoid the template.
- Anything that reads as "an agency made this for me" — overly polished, formal, or impersonal. The site should feel like Eric built it, not like a Webflow template.
- Gradient text, hero-metric templates, identical card grids, numbered eyebrows on every section.

## Design Principles

1. **Personality in the margins.** The structure can be spare and precise; that's what makes the human details land. The intro panel, Eric's Corner, and small touches (CNRL chip, winking nav character) are where Eric actually shows up — protect them.
2. **Earn every element.** Nothing decorative for its own sake. The embers, the redline motif, the glass surfaces — each earns its place by adding atmosphere without asking for attention.
3. **Two-speed reading.** Recruiters skim; engineers read. Structure the content so the three-second skim gives a complete picture, and there's depth underneath for anyone who wants it.
4. **Dark as a deliberate choice.** Not dark "because tools look cool dark." The dark field and ember glow create a specific mood — late-night, focused, a little alive. Preserve that when extending the site.
5. **Confidence without performance.** No hero metrics, no skill bars, no "I'm a passionate developer" copy. The work speaks; the copy is dry and honest.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA as a baseline. `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce` is already respected throughout — all new animations must continue to honor it. Standard keyboard and screen reader compatibility expected.
